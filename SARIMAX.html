SARIMA

An extension to ARIMA that supports the direct modeling of the seasonal component of the series is called SARIMA. Annual changes are great for building long term forecasting models, but using them may cause us to miss out on the granularity necessary for understanding the here and now. So instead of transforming the Y variable of our regression into an annual change, we can directly control for seasonality by including it as a feature (X variable) in our model. That’s what SARIMA does. SARIMA stands for Seasonal Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average (quite a mouthful). It’s very much like ARIMA but more powerful. SARIMAX is used on data sets that have seasonal cycles.
